duploid

A preduploid \(\mathcal{D}\) is called a duploid when it satisfies the following properties:

  • the preduploid \(\mathcal{D}\) is univalent;
  • every positive object \(P\in{}\mathcal{D}\) has an upshift;
  • every negative object of \(N\in{}\mathcal{D}\) has a downshift.
