deductive system

A deductive-system \(\mathcal{D}\) is given by a type \(\mathbf{ob}_{\mathcal{D}}\) of “objects”, and for every \(A,B\in\mathbf{ob}_{\mathcal{D}}\) a set \(\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(A,B\right)\) of “morphisms”, together with cut and identity laws:

  1. cut: for each \(A,B,C\in\mathbf{ob}_{\mathcal{D}}\) and \(f\in\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(A,B\right)\) and \(g\in\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(B,C\right)\), a morphism \(f;g\in\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(A,C\right)\);
  2. identity: for each \(A\in\mathbf{ob}_{\mathcal{D}}\), a morphism \(\mathbf{idn}_{A}\in\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(A,A\right)\) such that \(\mathbf{idn}_{A};f=f=f;\mathbf{idn}_{B}\) for each \(f\in\mathbf{hom}_{\mathcal{D}}\left(A,B\right)\).
